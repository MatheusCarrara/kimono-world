Types of gi

there are several types of gi, the price of which varies. this price variation can occur between one sport and another, or just by the type of gi for the same martial art.



karate gi

Karate gi's generally have a straighter cut and are fitted to the body, with shorter, tighter sleeves. This allows greater freedom to execute the kicks, punches and movements characteristic of this martial art.

the price of this type of kimono varies between:

$17,20 e $202,60

judo gi

The official colors of judo kimonos are white and blue. White is the most traditional color in Japanese culture and has been used since the beginning of the sport. The blue color, on the other hand, is compulsory in official competitions.

the price of this type of kimono varies between:

$25,54 e $65,24

jiu-jitsu gi

Jiu gi's are closer to the body than Judo's (also called Judogis) and in Jiu, the gi's are (and can, except for competitions) be more decorated with patches and brand logos, as well as having a much wider variety of colors than Judogis.

the price of this type of kimono varies between:

$19,25 e $94,21

original Japanese kimono

Generally made of silk, the designs, colors and prints vary according to the circumstance and marital status of the wearer. Single women wear the furissode, a kimono with long, wide sleeves. Married women wear the tomessode with ordinary sleeves.

the price of this type of kimono usually starts at around:

5000 yenes, ou $50,65
